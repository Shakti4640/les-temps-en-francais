TENSE 17: L'IMPÉRATIF PRÉSENT (Present Imperative)

📦 SECTION SUMMARY

What it is: The imperative mood used for commands, orders, requests, advice, instructions, invitations, and prohibitions. It exists in only 3 forms (tu, nous, vous) and is used without a subject pronoun.

When to USE it: Direct commands ("Eat!"), polite requests ("Please sit down"), advice ("Be careful"), suggestions ("Let's go!"), instructions (recipes, manuals), directions ("Turn left"), encouragement ("Don't give up!"), prohibitions ("Don't touch!").

When NOT to use it: For polite/softened requests (use conditionnel instead), for indirect commands (use subjonctif), for 3rd person commands (use subjonctif: qu'il parte!), when you want to sound less authoritative.

Formation: Same as présent de l'indicatif BUT: (1) no subject pronoun, (2) -ER verbs drop the -s in the tu form, (3) four verbs are irregular (être, avoir, savoir, vouloir).

Special Features: Object pronouns go AFTER the verb in affirmative commands (with hyphens) but BEFORE the verb in negative commands.

Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐ (Beginner-Intermediate — easy forms, tricky pronoun placement)

1️⃣ WHAT IS L'IMPÉRATIF?

The impératif (imperative) is not a tense — it's a mood (like indicatif, conditionnel, subjonctif). It expresses the speaker's will directed at someone else: commands, requests, advice, suggestions, instructions.

Key Characteristics:

FeatureDetail
Number of formsOnly 3 (tu, nous, vous)
Subject pronounNONE — dropped entirely
Who can you command?Only people you're talking TO (tu/vous) or including yourself (nous)
3rd person commands?Use subjunctive: Qu'il parte ! (Let him leave!)
Exists in how many tenses?2: Impératif présent (this one) and Impératif passé (Tense 18)

The Three Imperative Persons:

FormWho you're addressingEnglish equivalent
Tu (2nd sg.)One person you know well (informal)"Eat!" (to a friend/child)
Nous (1st pl.)Yourself + others ("let's")"Let's eat!"
Vous (2nd pl./formal)Multiple people OR one person (formal)"Eat!" (formal/plural)

💡 There is NO imperative for je, il/elle, or ils/elles!

  • You can't command yourself in the 1st person singular
  • You can't directly command someone who isn't present (3rd person) — use subjunctive instead

2️⃣ FORMATION (HOW TO CONJUGATE)

The Golden Rule:

The imperative looks (almost) exactly like the present indicative — just remove the subject pronoun!

🔷 Group 1: -ER Verbs

Model: PARLER (to speak)

PersonPrésent IndicatifImpératif⚠️ Change?

Nousnous parlonsParlons !No change
Vousvous parlezParlez !No change

🔴 THE #1 IMPERATIVE RULE: For -ER verbs, the tu form drops the final -s!

  • Indicatif: tu parles → Impératif: Parle ! (NOT Parles !)

More -ER Examples:

VerbTuNousVous

💡 WHY drop the -s? Historical reasons — Old French -ER verbs didn't have an -s in the tu form. The imperative preserved the older form. This also applies to verbs that conjugate like -ER verbs: ouvrir → Ouvre !, offrir → Offre !, souffrir → Souffre !, cueillir → Cueille !, couvrir → Couvre !

⚠️ THE EXCEPTION TO THE EXCEPTION: -S Returns Before Y and EN!

When the tu imperative of an -ER verb is followed by y or en, the -s comes back for pronunciation (liaison):

Without y/enWith y/enWhy?
Mange !Manges-en ! (Eat some!)-s returns for liaison /mɑ̃ʒ‿zɑ̃/
Parle !Parles-en ! (Talk about it!)-s returns
Va ! (Go!)Vas-y ! (Go ahead!)-s returns for liaison /va‿zi/
Pense !Penses-y ! (Think about it!)-s returns
Retourne !Retournes-y ! (Go back there!)-s returns

🔴 This is ONLY for pronunciation purposes — you need the /z/ sound for smooth liaison before the vowels of y and en.

3️⃣ REFLEXIVE VERBS IN THE IMPERATIVE

Reflexive verbs have special pronoun behavior in the imperative:

se préparer (to get ready)Prépare-toi !Préparons-nous !Préparez-vous !

Negative: Pronoun goes BEFORE the verb (normal position)

toi → te returns in negative imperatives!

AffirmativeNegative
Lève-toi ! (Get up!)Ne te lève pas ! (Don't get up!)
Assieds-toi ! (Sit down!)Ne t'assieds pas ! (Don't sit down!)
Dépêche-toi ! (Hurry up!)Ne te dépêche pas ! (Don't hurry!)
Tais-toi ! (Be quiet!)Ne te tais pas ! (Don't be quiet!)
Calme-toi ! (Calm down!)Ne te calme pas ! (Don't calm down!)

Asseyez-vous ! (Sit down!)Ne vous asseyez pas ! (Don't sit down!)

🔴 KEY PATTERN:

  • Affirmative: Verb - pronoun (after) → Lève-toi !
  • Negative: Ne + pronoun + verb + pas (before) → Ne te lève pas !

4️⃣ OBJECT PRONOUNS WITH THE IMPERATIVE

This is one of the trickiest aspects of the French imperative. Object pronouns change position depending on whether the command is affirmative or negative.

🔷 AFFIRMATIVE: Pronouns go AFTER the verb (with hyphens)

Structure: Verb - pronoun(s) !

IndicatifImpératif (Affirmative)English
Tu le regardes.Regarde-le !Look at him/it!
Tu la prends.Prends-la !Take it/her!
Tu les écoutes.Écoute-les !Listen to them!
Tu lui parles.Parle-lui !Talk to him/her!
Tu leur écris.Écris-leur !Write to them!
Tu en manges.Manges-en ! ⚠️Eat some!
Tu y vas.Vas-y ! ⚠️Go ahead! / Go there!
Tu me donnes le livre.Donne-moi le livre ! ⚠️Give me the book!
Tu nous aides.Aide-nous !Help us!

⚠️ CRITICAL CHANGES in affirmative imperative:

  • me → moi (Donne-moi ! NOT Donne-me !)
  • te → toi (Lève-toi ! NOT Lève-te !)
  • BUT: me → m' before en/y (Donnez-m'en ! = Give me some!)
  • -s returns before en and y on -ER verbs (Manges-en ! Vas-y !)

🔷 NEGATIVE: Pronouns go BEFORE the verb (normal position)

Structure: Ne + pronoun(s) + verb + pas !

Impératif (Affirmative)Impératif (Negative)English
Regarde-le !Ne le regarde pas !Don't look at him!
Prends-la !Ne la prends pas !Don't take it!
Écoute-les !Ne les écoute pas !Don't listen to them!
Parle-lui !Ne lui parle pas !Don't talk to him/her!
Écris-leur !Ne leur écris pas !Don't write to them!
Manges-en !N'en mange pas !Don't eat any!
Vas-y !N'y va pas !Don't go there!
Donne-moi le livre !Ne me donne pas le livre !Don't give me the book!
Aide-nous !Ne nous aide pas !Don't help us!
Lève-toi !Ne te lève pas !Don't get up!

🔴 MASTER SUMMARY:

AFFIRMATIVE:  VERB - PRONOUN !     (Donne-le-moi !)
NEGATIVE:     NE PRONOUN VERB PAS ! (Ne me le donne pas !)

9️⃣ TECHNIQUES & MEMORY TIPS 🧠

Technique 1: The "-ER = No S" Rhyme

"ER verbs, lose the S — Mange, Parle, no more, no less!"

Remember: ONLY -ER verbs (and verbs conjugating like them) drop the -s. All others keep it.

Quick test:

  • Parler → tu parles → Parle ! ❌ no -s
  • Finir → tu finis → Finis ! ✅ keep -s
  • Prendre → tu prends → Prends ! ✅ keep -s
  • Ouvrir → tu ouvres → Ouvre ! ❌ no -s (conjugates like -ER)
  • Aller → tu vas → Va ! ❌ no -s (irregular -ER)

Technique 2: The "BEFORE/AFTER" Pronoun Rule

POSITIVE COMMAND = pronoun AFTER  →  "Regarde-LE !"
NEGATIVE COMMAND = pronoun BEFORE →  "Ne LE regarde pas !"

Think: POSITIVE = PUSH pronoun after the verb
       NEGATIVE = PULL pronoun before the verb

Technique 3: The "ME/TE → MOI/TOI" Switch

In affirmative commands only:

  • me → moi (Donne-moi !)
  • te → toi (Lève-toi !)

In negative commands, they go back to normal:

  • Ne me donne pas !
  • Ne te lève pas !

Think of it this way: When the pronoun comes AFTER the verb (affirmative), it's stressed → stressed forms = moi, toi. When it comes BEFORE (negative), it's unstressed → me, te.

Technique 4: The "Four Rebels" Memory Device

The four verbs with truly irregular imperatives all come from the subjunctive:

"SAVE" = Savoir, Avoir, Vouloir, Être

VerbTuNousVousMnemonic
SavoirSacheSachonsSachez"Sache it!"
AvoirAieAyonsAyez"Aie courage!"
Vouloir(Veuille)(Veuillons)Veuillez"Veuillez, please!"
ÊtreSoisSoyonsSoyez"Sois brave!"

Technique 5: Daily Practice — Command Yourself!

Narrate commands to yourself throughout the day:

"Réveille-toi ! Lève-toi ! Prends une douche ! Habille-toi ! Prépare le petit-déjeuner ! Mange ! Brosse-toi les dents ! Dépêche-toi ! Prends tes clés ! Ferme la porte ! Va au travail ! Concentre-toi ! Travaille bien ! Fais une pause ! Mange ton déjeuner ! Rentre à la maison ! Détends-toi ! Prépare le dîner ! Couche-toi ! Dors bien !"

Technique 6: The "Y and EN Restore the S" Exception

Normal:     Mange !     (no -s)
+ EN:       Manges-en ! (s returns!)

Normal:     Va !        (no -s)
+ Y:        Vas-y !     (s returns!)

WHY? → You need the /z/ sound for liaison before vowels

Surveillez la couleur — quand c'est bien doré, sortez la quiche du four. Laissez-la reposer cinq minutes avant de couper. Et surtout, régalez-vous !
