TENSE 11: LE CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT (Present Conditional)

📦 SECTION SUMMARY

What it is: The conditionnel présent expresses actions that would happen under certain conditions, polite requests, wishes, dreams, advice, unconfirmed information, and the future as seen from a past perspective. It is one of the most versatile and frequently used moods in French.

When to USE it:

  • Polite requests → Je voudrais un café (I would like a coffee)
  • Hypothetical / unreal conditions → Si j'étais riche, j'achèterais un château (If I were rich, I would buy a castle)
  • Wishes and dreams → J'aimerais voyager (I would love to travel)
  • Advice and suggestions → Tu devrais dormir plus (You should sleep more)
  • Future in the past → Il a dit qu'il viendrait (He said he would come)
  • Unconfirmed information / rumors → Le président serait malade (The president is reportedly sick)
  • Softened statements → Je dirais que c'est possible (I would say it's possible)
  • Imaginary scenarios → Ce serait génial ! (That would be great!)

When NOT to use it:

  • After si (if) — NEVER conditionnel after si
  • For real/likely conditions (use présent + futur simple)
  • For definite future actions (use futur simple or futur proche)
  • When a fact is confirmed (use indicative tenses)
  • After expressions requiring subjunctive (use subjonctif)

Formation: Futur simple STEM + Imparfait ENDINGS (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient)

English equivalent: "would do," "would eat," "would go," "could," "should"

Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐ (Easy to form if you know futur simple — the USES are what require mastery)

1️⃣ WHAT IS LE CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT?

The conditionnel is technically considered a mood (like indicative or subjunctive), though some grammarians classify it as a tense within the indicative mood. Regardless of classification, it is absolutely essential to master.

At its core, the conditionnel expresses something that is not certain, not real, or dependent on something else. It's the mood of possibility, imagination, politeness, and speculation.

The Core Concept:

INDICATIVE = What IS / What WAS / What WILL BE (reality)
CONDITIONAL = What WOULD BE / What COULD BE (possibility, imagination)

Think of It as a "Softener":

Direct / Certain (Indicative)Softened / Conditional
Je veux un café. (I want a coffee.)Je voudrais un café. (I would like a coffee.)
Tu dois partir. (You must leave.)Tu devrais partir. (You should leave.)
C'est vrai. (It's true.)Ce serait vrai. (It would be true. / It's reportedly true.)
Je peux t'aider. (I can help you.)Je pourrais t'aider. (I could help you.)

💡 KEY INSIGHT: The conditionnel takes the "edge" off statements. It makes demands into requests, facts into possibilities, and certainties into hypotheses. It's the diplomatic mood of French.

2️⃣ FORMATION (HOW TO CONJUGATE)

The Golden Formula:

CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT = FUTUR SIMPLE STEM + IMPARFAIT ENDINGS

This is one of the most elegant formulas in French grammar. If you already know:

  • The stem of the futur simple (the infinitive or irregular stem)
  • The endings of the imparfait (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient)

...then you already know the conditionnel!

⚠️ PRONUNCIATION TRAP: For the je form:

  • Futur simple: je parlerai = /paʁ.lə.ʁe/ (sounds like -é)
  • Conditionnel: je parlerais = /paʁ.lə.ʁɛ/ (sounds like -è)

In many regions of France, these two sounds have merged, making je parlerai and je parlerais sound identical! Context is your only clue. However, in careful/formal speech and in many francophone regions (Belgium, Switzerland, Canada, southern France), the distinction is maintained:

  • -ai = /e/ (closed, like "ay")
  • -ais = /ɛ/ (open, like "eh")

3️⃣ WHEN TO USE LE CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT ✅

Use #1: POLITENESS (The "Please" Mood)

The single most common everyday use. The conditionnel transforms blunt demands into polite requests.

Basic Polite Requests:

Blunt (Présent)Polite (Conditionnel)English
Je veux un café.Je voudrais un café.I would like a coffee.
Tu peux m'aider ?Tu pourrais m'aider ?Could you help me?
Vous avez l'heure ?Vous auriez l'heure ?Would you have the time?
Je veux parler au directeur.Je voudrais parler au directeur.I would like to speak to the manager.
Tu peux fermer la porte ?Tu pourrais fermer la porte ?Could you close the door?
On peut avoir l'addition ?On pourrait avoir l'addition ?Could we have the check?

💡 THE POLITENESS SCALE:

  1. Je veux un café. (I want — blunt, almost rude)
  2. Je voudrais un café. (I would like — polite ✅)
  3. J'aimerais un café. (I would love — very polite ✅✅)
  4. Pourriez-vous me donner un café ? (Could you give me — very formal ✅✅✅)

In Restaurants, Shops, and Services:

French (Conditionnel)English
Je voudrais une baguette, s'il vous plaît.I would like a baguette, please.
Je prendrais le menu du jour.I would have the set menu.
Nous aimerions une table pour deux.We would like a table for two.
Est-ce que je pourrais essayer cette robe ?Could I try on this dress?
Auriez-vous ce livre en stock ?Would you have this book in stock?
Je souhaiterais prendre rendez-vous.I would like to make an appointment.
Pourriez-vous me montrer le chemin ?Could you show me the way?
Sauriez-vous où se trouve la gare ?Would you know where the station is?

💡 TECHNIQUE: The "Would" Test for Politeness
If you can add "would" or "could" in English to make it more polite, use conditionnel in French.

Use #2: HYPOTHETICAL / UNREAL CONDITIONS (Si + Imparfait → Conditionnel)

🔴 THIS IS THE MOST TESTED USE IN EXAMS!

The conditionnel is used in the result clause of hypothetical (unreal/unlikely) conditions.

The Structure:

SI + IMPARFAIT (condition — unreal) → CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT (result — would happen)

⚠️ THE IRON RULE REPEATED: The conditionnel goes in the RESULT clause, NEVER after si!

  • ✅ Si j'avais le temps, je voyagerais. (If I had time, I would travel.)
  • ❌ Si je voyagerais... (NEVER!)

Complete Examples:

SI + ImparfaitConditionnel (Result)English
Si j'étais riche,j'achèterais un château.If I were rich, I would buy a castle.
Si tu avais le temps,tu pourrais venir.If you had time, you could come.
Si elle parlait français,elle comprendrait ce film.If she spoke French, she would understand this film.
Si nous habitions à Paris,nous irions au musée chaque semaine.If we lived in Paris, we would go to the museum every week.
Si vous étiez président,que feriez-vous ?If you were president, what would you do?
Si ils gagnaient au loto,ils feraient le tour du monde.If they won the lottery, they would travel around the world.
Si je savais la réponse,je te le dirais.If I knew the answer, I would tell you.
Si on pouvait voler,la vie serait différente.If we could fly, life would be different.
Si tu me demandais,je viendrais tout de suite.If you asked me, I would come right away.
Si le monde était parfait,il n'y aurait pas de guerre.If the world were perfect, there would be no war.

💡 NUANCE — Real vs. Unreal:

TypeStructureExampleMeaning
Real/Possible (Type 1)Si + présent → futurSi j'ai le temps, je viendrai.If I have time (maybe I will), I'll come.
Unreal/Unlikely (Type 2)Si + imparfait → conditionnelSi j'avais le temps, je viendrais.If I had time (but I don't), I would come.

The imparfait + conditionnel combination implies the condition is contrary to reality or very unlikely.

The Order Can Be Reversed:

Normal OrderReversed Order

Si tu venais, je serais content.Je serais content si tu venais.
Si nous avions le temps, nous voyagerions.Nous voyagerions si nous avions le temps.

💡 When the result clause comes first, there's no comma. When the si-clause comes first, use a comma before the result.

Use #5: FUTURE IN THE PAST (Futur du Passé)

🔴 THIS IS EXTREMELY IMPORTANT AND OFTEN OVERLOOKED!

When reporting what someone said about the future, but the reporting is in the past, the futur simple becomes conditionnel présent. This is called the "future in the past" (futur dans le passé).

The Rule:

DIRECT SPEECH (what they actually said):
    "Je VIENDRAI demain." (I WILL come tomorrow.) — futur simple

INDIRECT SPEECH (reported in the past):
    Il a dit qu'il VIENDRAIT le lendemain. (He said he WOULD come the next day.) — conditionnel

The Shift Pattern:

Direct Speech (Futur Simple)Indirect Speech Past (Conditionnel)
"Je partirai."Il a dit qu'il partirait.
"will leave""said he would leave"
"Nous viendrons."Ils ont dit qu'ils viendraient.
"will come""said they would come"
"Il pleuvra."On a annoncé qu'il pleuvrait.
"will rain""announced it would rain"

Complete Examples:

Direct SpeechReported Speech (Past)
"Je ferai les courses."Il a dit qu'il ferait les courses. (He said he would do the shopping.)
"Nous arriverons à 8h."Ils ont promis qu'ils arriveraient à 8h. (They promised they would arrive at 8.)
"Tu aimeras ce film."Elle m'a dit que j'aimerais ce film. (She told me I would like this film.)
"Il fera beau demain."La météo a annoncé qu'il ferait beau le lendemain. (The forecast announced it would be nice the next day.)
"Je ne pourrai pas venir."Il a expliqué qu'il ne pourrait pas venir. (He explained he wouldn't be able to come.)
"Nous déménagerons en juin."Ils nous ont dit qu'ils déménageraient en juin. (They told us they would move in June.)
"Je t'appellerai ce soir."Elle a promis qu'elle m'appellerait ce soir-là. (She promised she would call me that evening.)
"Ça sera difficile."Il savait que ça serait difficile. (He knew it would be difficult.)

💡 THE CONCORDANCE DES TEMPS (Sequence of Tenses):

When the main verb is in a past tense, the future becomes conditionnel:

Main Verb TenseSubordinate Clause
Present: Il dit que......il viendra. (futur simple)
Past: Il a dit que......il viendrait. (conditionnel)
Present: Je sais que......tu réussiras. (futur simple)
Past: Je savais que......tu réussirais. (conditionnel)
Present: Elle pense que......ce sera facile. (futur simple)
Past: Elle pensait que......ce serait facile. (conditionnel)

Use #6: UNCONFIRMED INFORMATION / RUMORS / REPORTED SPEECH (Journalistic Conditional)

🔴 A distinctive French use with no exact English parallel!

In journalism and formal reporting, the conditionnel signals that information is not confirmed — it's rumored, alleged, or reported by a source that hasn't been verified.

French (Conditionnel)English EquivalentMeaning
Le président serait malade.The president is reportedly/allegedly sick.Unconfirmed

💡 WHY THIS MATTERS: Using the conditionnel in this way protects the speaker/writer from making a false claim. It signals: "I'm reporting this, but I'm not personally confirming it's true."

Signal phrases often used with this:

  • Selon (according to) + source
  • D'après (according to) + source
  • Il paraîtrait que (it would appear that)
  • Apparemment (apparently) — though this can also be used with indicative

⚠️ IMPORTANT FOR LANGUAGE LEARNERS: This use is very common in French news. When you hear/read the conditionnel in a news report, it doesn't mean "would" — it means "reportedly/allegedly."

4️⃣ WHEN NOT TO USE LE CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT ❌

❌ Mistake #1: Using Conditionnel After SI (The Cardinal Sin!)

❌ WRONG✅ CORRECTRule
Si je voudrais, je partirais.Si je voulais, je partirais.After si → imparfait (NOT conditionnel!)
Si tu pourrais venir...Si tu pouvais venir...After si → imparfait
Si elle serait riche...Si elle était riche...After si → imparfait
Si nous aurions le temps...Si nous avions le temps...After si → imparfait
Si je saurais la réponse...Si je savais la réponse...After si → imparfait

🔴 THIS IS THE #1 MOST COMMON ERROR IN FRENCH — even native speakers sometimes make it!

THE RULE: "Les SI n'aiment pas les -RAIS" (The SI's don't like the -RAIS)
This is a famous French grammar saying. SI is never followed by conditionnel (-rais endings).

⚠️ EXCEPTION — SI meaning "WHETHER":
When si means "whether" (not "if"), it CAN be followed by conditionnel:

  • Je me demande si elle viendrait. (I wonder whether she would come.) ✅
  • Il ne sait pas si ça marcherait. (He doesn't know whether it would work.) ✅

This works because si here introduces an indirect question, not a condition.

❌ Mistake #2: Using Conditionnel for Certain/Definite Future Actions

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Demain, j'irais au cinéma. (if it's a plan)Demain, j'irai au cinéma.Definite plan → futur simple
L'année prochaine, nous déménagerions. (if decided)L'année prochaine, nous déménagerons.Decided future → futur simple

💡 THE DISTINCTION:

  • Futur simple = WILL happen (certainty/plan)

  • Conditionnel = WOULD happen (possibility/condition)

  • Je partirai demain. = I will leave tomorrow. (It's decided.)

  • Je partirais demain... = I would leave tomorrow... (if something were different)

❌ Mistake #3: Using Conditionnel When Indicative Is Needed (After Confirmed Facts)

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Je sais qu'il viendrait. (present knowledge)Je sais qu'il viendra.Current knowledge about the future → futur simple
Il est certain que ça marcherait.Il est certain que ça marchera.Certainty → indicative (futur)

💡 When the main clause expresses certainty (je sais, il est certain, c'est sûr), the subordinate clause uses indicative (futur simple), NOT conditionnel.

BUT when the main clause is in the past, conditionnel is correct:

  • Je savais qu'il viendrait. ✅ (I knew he would come — future in the past)

❌ Mistake #4: Confusing Conditionnel with Subjunctive Triggers

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Je veux qu'il viendrait.Je veux qu'il vienne.Vouloir que → subjunctive
Il faut qu'on ferait ça.Il faut qu'on fasse ça.Il faut que → subjunctive
Je souhaite qu'elle serait heureuse.Je souhaite qu'elle soit heureuse.Souhaiter que → subjunctive

💡 After expressions of desire, necessity, emotion, doubt + que, use subjunctive, NOT conditionnel.
Conditionnel is for independent clauses or result clauses, not after subjunctive triggers.

❌ Mistake #5: Using Conditionnel in Time Clauses (Quand + Future)

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Quand je serais grand, je serai pompier.Quand je serai grand, je serai pompier.After quand (future) → futur simple
Lorsque tu arriverais, appelle-moi.Lorsque tu arriveras, appelle-moi.After lorsque (future) → futur simple

💡 Remember: after quand, lorsque, dès que, aussitôt que in future contexts, use futur simple (or futur antérieur), NOT conditionnel.

9️⃣ TECHNIQUES & MEMORY TIPS 🧠

Technique 1: The "Futur + Imparfait Baby" Visualization

    FUTUR SIMPLE          IMPARFAIT
    (stem/father)    +    (endings/mother)
         ↓                     ↓
    ┌─────────────────────────────┐
    │   CONDITIONNEL PRÉSENT      │
    │   (their baby!)             │
    │   parler- + -ais            │
    │   = parlerais               │
    └─────────────────────────────┘

The conditionnel is literally the child of the futur simple and the imparfait — it inherits the stem from one parent and the endings from the other!

Technique 2: The "-R-" Detector

Whenever you see or hear a -R- sound before the ending:

  • -R- + futur endings (-ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont) → Futur Simple
  • -R- + imparfait endings (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient) → Conditionnel
  • NO -R- + imparfait endings → Imparfait
je parlais     = imparfait    (no -R-)
je parlerai    = futur simple (-R- + -ai)
je parlerais   = conditionnel (-R- + -ais)

Technique 3: The "Les SI n'aiment pas les -RAIS" Mantra

Repeat this famous French saying until it's automatic:

"Les SI n'aiment pas les -RAIS"
(The SIs don't like the -RAIS)

This means: NEVER put conditionnel after SI (when si = if).

Write it on a sticky note. Put it on your mirror. Tattoo it on your brain.

Technique 4: The "Would" Translation Test

When speaking/writing, if the English sentence uses:

  • "would" → probably conditionnel ✅
  • "could" → probably pourrais (conditionnel of pouvoir) ✅
  • "should" → probably devrais (conditionnel of devoir) ✅
  • "would like" → voudrais or aimerais ✅

But be careful:

  • "would" as a past habit ("I would go every day") → imparfait in French, NOT conditionnel!
    • Quand j'étais petit, j'allais au parc tous les jours. (imparfait!)

Technique 5: The "Daydream" Practice

Practice the conditionnel by daydreaming out loud in French:

Si j'étais millionnaire, j'achèterais une île. J'inviterais tous mes amis. On mangerait des fruits tropicaux. On nagerait dans la mer tous les jours. Ce serait le paradis. Je ne travaillerais plus jamais...

This is fun AND effective practice!

Technique 6: Recognizing "Future in the Past" in Reading

When you see a conditionnel form in a narrative text and it doesn't seem hypothetical, ask yourself: "Is someone reporting what was said/thought/expected in the past?"

TextAnalysis
Il a promis qu'il viendrait.Not hypothetical — this is future in the past. He promised he "would come."
Elle savait qu'ils comprendraient.Future in the past. She knew they "would understand."

Technique 7: The "Newspaper Conditional" Awareness

When reading French news and you see conditionnel where you'd expect a fact:

  • Don't translate as "would"
  • Translate as "reportedly" or "allegedly"

1️⃣3️⃣ THE "WOULD" TRAP: ENGLISH "WOULD" ≠ ALWAYS CONDITIONNEL

This is crucial. English "would" has THREE different meanings, and only ONE of them translates to French conditionnel:

English "Would"French TenseExample
Hypothetical/conditional "would"Conditionnel ✅I would go if I could. → J'irais si je pouvais.
Polite "would"Conditionnel ✅I would like a coffee. → Je voudrais un café.
Past habit "would" (= used to)Imparfait ⚠️Every summer, we would go to the beach. → Chaque été, nous allions à la plage.

🔴 THE TRAP: "When I was young, I would play outside every day."

  • ❌ Quand j'étais jeune, je jouerais dehors tous les jours.
  • ✅ Quand j'étais jeune, je jouais dehors tous les jours. (IMPARFAIT!)

HOW TO TEST: If you can replace "would" with "used to" and the meaning doesn't change, it's a past habit → use imparfait.

  • "I would go" = "I used to go" → imparfait ✅
  • "I would go (if I could)" ≠ "I used to go" → conditionnel ✅
