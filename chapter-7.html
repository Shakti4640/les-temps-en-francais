Le Futur Proche

Immediate / near future, plans about to happen

TENSE 7: LE FUTUR PROCHE (The Near/Immediate Future)

📦 SECTION SUMMARY

What it is: The "going to" future — expresses actions that are about to happen, planned, or intended. It's the most common way to express the future in spoken French.

When to USE it: Imminent actions ("I'm about to leave"), plans and intentions ("I'm going to study tonight"), near-future events, predictions based on current evidence, warnings, instructions in informal contexts, expressing certainty about the future.

When NOT to use it: Formal writing/speeches (use futur simple), distant/uncertain future events (prefer futur simple), hypothetical conditions (use conditionnel), after quand/lorsque/dès que referring to the future (use futur simple).

Formation: ALLER (present tense) + INFINITIVE — the simplest compound structure in French!

Key Insight: This is the #1 most used future tense in spoken French. Many French speakers use it almost exclusively instead of the futur simple in daily conversation.

Difficulty Level: ⭐ (Beginner — extremely easy to form and use!)

1️⃣ WHAT IS LE FUTUR PROCHE?

The futur proche (literally "near future") expresses future actions using the construction "going to + verb" — exactly like English! It's the most natural and common way to talk about the future in everyday spoken French.

The Concept:

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                                  ║
║  ENGLISH:   I am GOING TO eat.                                  ║
║  FRENCH:    Je VAIS manger.                                     ║
║                                                                  ║
║  ENGLISH:   She is GOING TO leave.                              ║
║  FRENCH:    Elle VA partir.                                     ║
║                                                                  ║
║  ENGLISH:   We are GOING TO study.                              ║
║  FRENCH:    Nous ALLONS étudier.                                ║
║                                                                  ║
║  SAME structure. SAME logic. SAME usage.                        ║
║  This is one of the EASIEST tenses for English speakers!        ║
║                                                                  ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

English Equivalents:

French (Futur Proche)English
Je vais mangerI am going to eat / I 'm gonna eat
Il va pleuvoirIt is going to rain / It 's gonna rain
Nous allons partirWe are going to leave

💡 KEY INSIGHT: The futur proche in French corresponds almost perfectly to "going to + verb" in English. If you would say "going to" in English, you can almost always use futur proche in French.

Special Case: "ALLER" + "ALLER" (Going to Go)

What happens when you want to say "I'm going to go"? You use aller twice:

FrenchEnglish
Je vais aller au cinéma.I'm going to go to the cinema.
Tu vas aller chez le médecin ?Are you going to go to the doctor?
Nous allons aller en France cet été.We're going to go to France this summer.
Ils vont aller à la plage.They're going to go to the beach.

💡 Yes, "je vais aller" sounds a bit repetitive, and in casual speech many people just say "je vais au cinéma" (using present for near future). But "je vais aller" is perfectly correct grammatically.

5️⃣ WHEN TO USE LE FUTUR PROCHE ✅

Use #1: Imminent Actions (About to Happen RIGHT NOW)

Something that is on the verge of happening — very near in time.

FrenchEnglish
Attention, tu vas tomber !Careful, you're going to fall!
Le film va commencer.The movie is about to start.
Dépêche-toi, le bus va partir !Hurry up, the bus is about to leave!
Je vais éternuer... Atchoum !I'm going to sneeze... Achoo!
Regarde les nuages — il va pleuvoir.Look at the clouds — it's going to rain.
Elle va accoucher d'un moment à l'autre.She's going to give birth any moment now.
Le vase va tomber !The vase is going to fall!
Chut ! Le bébé va se réveiller.Shh! The baby is going to wake up.

💡 IMMINENT SIGNAL WORDS: bientôt (soon), tout de suite (right away), dans un instant (in a moment), d'un moment à l'autre (any moment now), maintenant (now — for something about to start).

Use #2: Plans and Intentions

Something you have decided to do or plan to do.

FrenchEnglish
Ce soir, je vais regarder un film.Tonight, I'm going to watch a movie.
Nous allons déménager en septembre.We're going to move in September.
Elle va étudier la médecine.She's going to study medicine.
Ils vont se marier l'année prochaine.They're going to get married next year.
Je vais apprendre le japonais.I'm going to learn Japanese.
Tu vas chercher un nouveau travail ?Are you going to look for a new job?
On va organiser une fête pour son anniversaire.We're going to organize a party for his birthday.
Nous allons acheter une maison.We're going to buy a house.
Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire pendant les vacances ?What are you going to do during the holidays?
Je vais prendre une année sabbatique.I'm going to take a sabbatical year.

💡 PLAN SIGNAL WORDS: ce soir (tonight), demain (tomorrow), la semaine prochaine (next week), l'année prochaine (next year), pendant les vacances (during the holidays), en septembre (in September), après le dîner (after dinner).

Use #3: Predictions Based on Current Evidence

When you can SEE or SENSE that something is about to happen based on what's happening NOW.

FrenchEnglishEvidence
Regarde ces nuages noirs — il va pleuvoir.Look at those black clouds — it's going to rain.You can see the clouds
Il est tout pâle — il va s'évanouir !He's very pale — he's going to faint!You can see his pallor
Avec cette circulation, on va être en retard.With this traffic, we're going to be late.You can see the traffic
Elle a beaucoup étudié — elle va réussir.She studied a lot — she's going to pass.You know she prepared
Ce projet va échouer si on ne change rien.This project is going to fail if we don't change anything.You can see the problems
Ça va être génial !It's going to be great!Based on what you know
Tu vas adorer ce restaurant.You're going to love this restaurant.Based on your knowledge of the person
Ils ne vont pas être contents.They're not going to be happy.Based on the situation

💡 THE EVIDENCE PRINCIPLE: Use futur proche when your prediction is based on something you can see, know, or observe RIGHT NOW. The present evidence points to the future result.

Compare with futur simple:

  • Il va pleuvoir. (I can see the clouds — evidence-based) — Futur proche ✅
  • Il pleuvra demain. (Weather forecast — no current evidence visible) — Futur simple ✅

Both are correct, but the nuance differs!

Use #4: Warnings and Threats

Alerting someone about an imminent consequence.

FrenchEnglish
Tu vas te faire mal !You're going to hurt yourself!
Attention, tu vas renverser ton verre !Careful, you're going to knock over your glass!

Ça va mal finir !This is going to end badly!

Use #5: Reassurance and Promises

Comforting someone or making informal promises.

FrenchEnglish
Ne t'inquiète pas, tout va bien se passer.Don't worry, everything is going to be fine.
Ça va aller, je te promets.It's going to be OK, I promise.
Tu vas voir, ça va être super.You'll see, it's going to be great.
Je vais t'aider, ne pleure pas.I'm going to help you, don't cry.
On va trouver une solution.We're going to find a solution.
Ça va passer, sois patient.It's going to pass, be patient.
Je ne vais pas t'abandonner.I'm not going to abandon you.
Tu vas guérir vite.You're going to get better quickly.

Use #6: Narrating Immediate Next Steps

Describing what you're about to do next, step by step.

FrenchEnglish
Bon, je vais vous expliquer le plan.OK, I'm going to explain the plan to you.
D'abord, on va manger, et après on va sortir.First, we're going to eat, and then we're going to go out.
Je vais commencer par une introduction.I'm going to start with an introduction.
Maintenant, je vais vous montrer comment ça marche.Now, I'm going to show you how it works.
Alors, voilà ce qu'on va faire...So, here's what we're going to do...
Je vais prendre une douche, et puis je vais m'habiller.I'm going to take a shower, and then I'm going to get dressed.
On va d'abord visiter le musée, ensuite on va déjeuner.We're going to visit the museum first, then we're going to have lunch.

Use #7: Expressing Strong Certainty About the Future

When you feel SURE something will happen.

FrenchEnglish
Tu vas réussir, j'en suis sûr !You're going to succeed, I'm sure of it!
Ce va être le meilleur concert de l'année.It's going to be the best concert of the year.
Ils vont gagner le match, c'est certain.They're going to win the match, it's certain.
Cette robe va te plaire.You're going to like this dress.
Ça va changer ta vie.It's going to change your life.
Ce film va cartonner au box-office.This movie is going to be a huge hit at the box office.

Use #8: Informal Instructions / Telling Someone What to Do

A softer way of giving instructions than the imperative.

Imperative (Direct)Futur Proche (Softer)English
Écoute !Tu vas écouter attentivement.You're going to listen carefully.
Prenez vos cahiers !Vous allez prendre vos cahiers.You're going to take out your notebooks.
Fais tes devoirs !Tu vas faire tes devoirs.You're going to do your homework.

💡 PARENT/TEACHER REGISTER: "Tu vas faire tes devoirs MAINTENANT" is a common way parents give firm instructions — it's an order disguised as a statement about the future.

Use #9: In Storytelling / Narration (Informal)

Using futur proche within a story to create anticipation.

FrenchEnglish
Et là, tu ne vas pas croire ce qui s'est passé...And there, you're not going to believe what happened...
Attends, je vais te raconter.Wait, I'm going to tell you.
Tu vas voir, c'est incroyable.You'll see, it's incredible.
Devine ce qui va se passer ensuite !Guess what's going to happen next!

6️⃣ WHEN NOT TO USE LE FUTUR PROCHE ❌

❌ Mistake #1: Using Futur Proche in Formal Writing/Speeches

❌ Too Informal✅ Formal (Futur Simple)Context
Le président va annoncer sa décision demain. (in formal journalism)Le président annoncera sa décision demain.Formal journalism
Nous allons examiner cette question. (in academic paper)Nous examinerons cette question.Academic writing
Cette loi va entrer en vigueur en janvier. (in legal text)Cette loi entrera en vigueur en janvier.Legal/official text
La cérémonie va commencer à 15 heures. (formal announcement)La cérémonie commencera à 15 heures.Official announcement

⚠️ NOTE: The futur proche is not "wrong" in these contexts — it's just less appropriate. In modern journalism, futur proche is increasingly accepted. But for truly formal contexts (legal, academic, presidential speeches), futur simple is preferred.

❌ Mistake #2: Using Futur Proche for Distant/Vague Future

❌ Less Natural✅ More Natural (Futur Simple)Why
Un jour, je vais devenir célèbre.Un jour, je deviendrai célèbre.Distant, uncertain future → futur simple feels more natural
Dans cent ans, les voitures vont voler.Dans cent ans, les voitures voleront.Very distant future → futur simple
Quand je vais être vieux...Quand je serai vieux...Distant future + "quand" → futur simple (see rule below!)

⚠️ NUANCE: These aren't strictly "wrong" — many French speakers DO use futur proche even for distant events. But futur simple is considered more appropriate for events that are:

  • Far in the future
  • Uncertain / hypothetical
  • Part of general predictions (not based on current evidence)
  • In formal register

❌ Mistake #3: Using Futur Proche After QUAND / LORSQUE / DÈS QUE (Future Context)

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Quand tu vas arriver, appelle-moi.Quand tu arriveras, appelle-moi.After quand/lorsque/dès que referring to the future → MUST use futur simple
Dès que je vais finir, je vais t'appeler.Dès que j'aurai fini, je t'appellerai.After dès que → futur simple or futur antérieur
Lorsque nous allons être prêts...Lorsque nous serons prêts...After lorsque → futur simple

🔴 CRITICAL RULE: After quand, lorsque, dès que, aussitôt que, tant que, une fois que in a future context, you MUST use the futur simple (or futur antérieur), NEVER futur proche. This is one of the biggest differences between English and French:

  • English: "When you arrive..." (present tense after "when")
  • French: "Quand tu arriveras..." (futur simple after "quand")
  • NOT: "Quand tu vas arriver..." (futur proche — WRONG after quand!)

We'll cover this in detail in the Futur Simple section.

❌ Mistake #4: Using Futur Proche for Hypothetical/Conditional Situations

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Si j'avais de l'argent, je vais acheter une maison.Si j'avais de l'argent, j'achèterais une maison.Hypothetical → conditionnel
À ta place, je vais accepter.À ta place, j'accepterais.Hypothetical advice → conditionnel

❌ Mistake #5: Using Futur Proche After SI (If-Clause for Future)

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Si tu vas venir, dis-le-moi.Si tu viens, dis-le-moi.After si → use PRESENT (never future!)
Si il va pleuvoir, on restera ici.**S'**il pleut, on restera ici.After si → present tense

🔴 REMINDER: After SI (if), you NEVER use any future tense (not futur proche, not futur simple):

  • ✅ Si tu viens... (présent)
  • ❌ Si tu vas venir... (futur proche — WRONG!)
  • ❌ Si tu viendras... (futur simple — WRONG!)

7️⃣ FUTUR PROCHE vs. FUTUR SIMPLE — PREVIEW COMPARISON

This is a crucial distinction we'll explore fully in the next tense, but here's an overview:

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                                      ║
║  FUTUR PROCHE                    vs.    FUTUR SIMPLE                ║
║  ─────────────                          ─────────────               ║
║  je vais manger                         je mangerai                  ║
║                                                                      ║
║  📱 Spoken / Informal                   📜 Written / Formal          ║
║  🔜 Near / Imminent                     🔮 Distant / General         ║
║  📋 Plans / Intentions                  🌐 Predictions / Promises    ║
║  👁️ Evidence-based                      💭 Hypothetical / Uncertain  ║
║  🗣️ Conversational                      📢 Speeches / Announcements ║
║  💯 Certain / Personal                  ❓ Less certain / Impersonal ║
║                                                                      ║
║  "I'm going to eat"                    "I will eat / I shall eat"   ║
║                                                                      ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

Quick Side-by-Side:

SituationFutur ProcheFutur SimplePreferred?
Plans for tonightJe vais manger au restaurant.Je mangerai au restaurant.FP (personal plan)
Imminent eventIl va pleuvoir !Il pleuvra.FP (you see the clouds)
Formal prediction—La population augmentera de 20%.FS (formal/statistical)
PromiseJe vais t'aider.Je t'aiderai.Both OK; FS slightly more solemn

💡 THE TREND IN MODERN FRENCH: The futur proche is gradually "eating" the futur simple's territory. In everyday spoken French, many speakers use futur proche for almost everything:

  • "Demain, je vais travailler." (instead of "je travaillerai")
  • "L'année prochaine, on va déménager." (instead of "on déménagera")

The futur simple survives mainly in:

  1. Formal contexts
  2. After quand/lorsque/dès que
  3. Very distant/uncertain futures
  4. Fixed expressions and proverbs

8️⃣ THE FUTUR PROCHE IN PAST CONTEXT (ALLER IN IMPARFAIT)

When you're narrating in the past and want to express what someone was going to do (future in the past), put aller in the imparfait:

╔═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗

║  PRESENT CONTEXT:   Je VAIS manger.                      ║
║                     (I'm going to eat.)                   ║
║                                                           ║
║  PAST CONTEXT:      J'ALLAIS manger.                     ║
║                     (I was going to eat.)                 ║
║                                                           ║
╚═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

Formation:

SubjectAller (Imparfait) + InfinitiveEnglish
Jeallais mangerI was going to eat
Tuallais partirYou were going to leave
Il/Elleallait comprendreHe/She was going to understand
Nousallions déménagerWe were going to move
Vousalliez voyagerYou were going to travel
Ils/Ellesallaient arriverThey were going to arrive

Uses of "Aller (Imparfait) + Infinitive":

A. Plans That DID Happen (Narrating):

FrenchEnglish
J'allais partir quand le téléphone a sonné.I was going to leave / I was about to leave when the phone rang.
Il allait dire quelque chose mais il a changé d'avis.He was going to say something but he changed his mind.
On allait manger quand les invités sont arrivés.We were about to eat when the guests arrived.

B. Plans That Did NOT Happen (Unfulfilled Intentions):

FrenchEnglish
J'allais t'appeler ! (mais tu as appelé en premier)I was going to call you! (but you called first)
Nous allions partir en vacances mais nous avons annulé.We were going to go on vacation but we cancelled.
Elle allait accepter le poste mais elle a trouvé mieux.She was going to accept the position but she found something better.
Il allait pleuvoir, mais finalement le soleil est revenu.It was going to rain, but finally the sun came back.

C. "Was About To" (Imminent Past):

FrenchEnglish
Le train allait partir quand j'ai sauté dedans.The train was about to leave when I jumped in.
J'allais m'endormir quand j'ai entendu un bruit.I was about to fall asleep when I heard a noise.
Elle allait tomber quand je l'ai rattrapée.She was about to fall when I caught her.
La bombe allait exploser — il ne restait que 10 secondes.The bomb was about to explode — there were only 10 seconds left.

D. In Reported Speech:

Direct SpeechReported Speech
« Je vais partir. »Il a dit qu'il allait partir. (He said he was going to leave.)
« Nous allons déménager. »Ils ont annoncé qu'ils allaient déménager. (They announced they were going to move.)
« Ça va être difficile. »Elle a prévenu que ça allait être difficile. (She warned it was going to be difficult.)

💡 REPORTED SPEECH TENSE SHIFT:

  • Futur proche (vais + infinitive) → allais + infinitive (aller in imparfait)
  • This follows the normal present → imparfait shift in reported speech.

🔟 TECHNIQUES & MEMORY TIPS 🧠

Technique 1: The "Going To" Direct Translation

This is the ONE French tense that translates almost perfectly from English:

I am going to eat    =    Je vais manger
She is going to come =    Elle va venir
We are going to see  =    Nous allons voir
They are going to do =    Ils vont faire

If you can say "going to + verb" in English → you can say "aller + infinitif" in French. It's that simple.

Technique 2: The "Proximity Scale"

Think of future tenses on a scale of proximity:

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                              ║
║  NOW ←──────────────────────────────────────────────→ DISTANT║
║                                                              ║
║  ├── FUTUR PROCHE ──┤                                        ║
║  │ (about to / going │                                       ║
║  │  to / plans)      │                                       ║
║  │                   │                                       ║
║  │                   ├──── FUTUR SIMPLE ─────────────────┤   ║
║  │                   │     (will / predictions /          │   ║
║  │                   │      formal / distant)             │   ║
║  │                   │                                    │   ║
║  ├─── OVERLAP ───────┤                                    │   ║
║  │   (both work      │                                    │   ║
║  │    for medium      │                                    │   ║
║  │    future)         │                                    │   ║
║                                                              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

The closer in time and the more personal/certain, the more likely futur proche. The more distant and formal/uncertain, the more likely futur simple.

Technique 3: The "Five Finger" Test for Choosing FP vs. FS

Hold up your hand. For each "yes" answer, fold a finger:

1. Is it happening SOON? (today, tonight, tomorrow) → YES = FP
2. Is it a PERSONAL plan/intention? → YES = FP
3. Can I SEE evidence it will happen? → YES = FP
4. Am I speaking INFORMALLY? → YES = FP
5. Am I feeling CERTAIN about it? → YES = FP

3+ fingers folded → FUTUR PROCHE
0-2 fingers folded → FUTUR SIMPLE

Technique 4: Practice with "Tomorrow's Schedule"

Every evening, describe what you're going to do tomorrow:

Demain, je vais me lever à 7 heures. Je vais prendre une douche et je vais m'habiller. Ensuite, je vais prendre le petit-déjeuner. À 8 heures, je vais partir au travail. Le matin, je vais avoir une réunion. À midi, je vais déjeuner avec un collègue. L'après-midi, je vais travailler sur mon projet. Le soir, je vais faire les courses et je vais préparer le dîner. Après le dîner, je vais regarder un épisode de ma série et je vais me coucher tôt.

Technique 5: The "Aller Stays, Infinitive Changes" Drill

Practice swapping infinitives rapidly while keeping the same subject:

Je vais manger → Je vais dormir → Je vais travailler → Je vais partir → Je vais lire → Je vais courir → Je vais écrire → Je vais voir → Je vais faire → Je vais prendre

Then switch subjects:

Tu vas manger → Il va manger → Nous allons manger → Vous allez manger → Ils vont manger
