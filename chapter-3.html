Chapter 3: TENSE 3: L'IMPARFAIT (The Imperfect Tense)

TENSE 3: L'IMPARFAIT (The Imperfect Tense)

📦 SECTION SUMMARY

What it is: The past tense used for ongoing situations, descriptions, backgrounds, habits, and states in the past. It paints the scenery of a past story.

When to USE it: Past descriptions (weather, appearance, emotions), past habits/routines, ongoing background actions, age/time in the past, politeness (softened requests), hypothetical si-clauses (unreal conditions), actions in progress when interrupted, repeated undefined past actions.

When NOT to use it: Completed/bounded actions (use passé composé), sequences of plot events (use passé composé), actions with specific time stamps (use passé composé), actions happening NOW (use présent).

Formation: Nous form of présent (minus -ons) + imparfait endings (-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient)

Key Insight: Only ONE irregular verb in the entire language: ÊTRE

Difficulty Level: ⭐⭐ for formation (very regular!) / ⭐⭐⭐⭐ for usage (knowing WHEN to use it vs. passé composé)

1️⃣ WHAT IS L'IMPARFAIT?

The imparfait is the descriptive past tense. While the passé composé tells you what happened (the events, the plot), the imparfait tells you what things were like (the scene, the backdrop, the atmosphere).

Think of it this way:

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                              ║
║  If your past story were a MOVIE:                            ║
║                                                              ║
║  🎬 PASSÉ COMPOSÉ = The ACTION scenes                        ║
║     (car chases, plot twists, explosions)                    ║
║                                                              ║
║  🎨 IMPARFAIT = The SETTING                                  ║
║     (establishing shots, background music,                   ║
║      character descriptions, the mood)                       ║
║                                                              ║
║  If your past story were a PAINTING:                         ║
║                                                              ║
║  🖼️ IMPARFAIT = The background, the sky, the landscape       ║
║  ✏️ PASSÉ COMPOSÉ = The figures, the action in the foreground ║
║                                                              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

English Equivalents:

French (Imparfait)English Possibilities
Je mangeaisI was eating / I used to eat / I ate (habitually) / I would eat (habitual)
Il pleuvaitIt was raining / It used to rain / It rained (ongoing)
Elle était contenteShe was happy (state/description)

💡 KEY INSIGHT: The imparfait has NO single English equivalent. It can translate as "was ___-ing," "used to ___," "would ___ (habitual)," or even simple past — depending on context. This is why it confuses English speakers!

2️⃣ FORMATION (HOW TO CONJUGATE)

The Golden Formula:

╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
║                                                              ║
║  STEP 1: Take the NOUS form of the PRESENT tense            ║
║  STEP 2: Remove the -ONS ending → this gives you the STEM   ║
║  STEP 3: Add the imparfait endings                           ║
║                                                              ║
║  Example: PARLER                                             ║
║  Nous parlONS → parl- → je parlais                           ║
║                                                              ║
╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝

🔴 THE ONE AND ONLY TRULY IRREGULAR VERB: ÊTRE

ÊTRE is the ONLY verb in French where you CANNOT use the nous present formula for the imparfait.

Nous sommes → stem would be "somm-" → ❌ This doesn't work!

Instead, the stem is ét- (unique to imparfait):

SubjectConjugation
J'étais
Tuétais
Il/Elle/Onétait
Nousétions
Vousétiez
Ils/Ellesétaient

💡 PRONUNCIATION: étais, étais, était, étaient all sound the same: /e.tɛ/. Étions = /e.tjɔ̃/, étiez = /e.tje/.

🔴 ÊTRE is by far the most used verb in the imparfait because it describes states of being, which is exactly what the imparfait is for! Memorize it cold.

Same structure as present tense — ne...pas wraps around the conjugated verb:

4️⃣ WHEN TO USE L'IMPARFAIT ✅

Use #1: Descriptions in the Past (Setting the Scene)

Describing what things looked like, felt like, sounded like — the atmosphere of a past moment.

Weather:

FrenchEnglish
Il faisait beau ce jour-là.The weather was nice that day.
Il pleuvait des cordes.It was raining cats and dogs.
Il neigeait et le vent soufflait fort.It was snowing and the wind was blowing hard.
Le soleil brillait dans un ciel bleu.The sun was shining in a blue sky.
Il faisait froid ce matin-là.It was cold that morning.
Le ciel était gris et menaçant.The sky was gray and threatening.

Physical Descriptions (People):

FrenchEnglish
Elle avait les cheveux longs et blonds.She had long blonde hair.
Il était grand et mince.He was tall and thin.
Le vieil homme portait un chapeau noir.The old man was wearing a black hat.
Elle avait les yeux verts et un sourire magnifique.She had green eyes and a magnificent smile.
Il mesurait presque deux mètres.He was almost two meters tall.
Le bébé avait les joues roses.The baby had pink cheeks.

Descriptions of Places:

FrenchEnglish
La maison était vieille et sentait le bois.The house was old and smelled of wood.
Les rues étaient vides et silencieuses.The streets were empty and silent.
Le jardin était rempli de fleurs.The garden was filled with flowers.
La pièce semblait très petite.The room seemed very small.
Le restaurant se trouvait au coin de la rue.The restaurant was on the corner of the street.
Les montagnes s'étendaient à l'horizon.The mountains stretched to the horizon.

Descriptions of Atmosphere:

FrenchEnglish
L'ambiance était chaleureuse.The atmosphere was warm/welcoming.
Tout semblait calme et paisible.Everything seemed calm and peaceful.
Une odeur de pain frais flottait dans l'air.A smell of fresh bread was floating in the air.
La musique résonnait dans toute la maison.Music was resonating throughout the house.
Il y avait beaucoup de monde dans la rue.There were many people in the street.
Ça sentait bon la lavande.It smelled nicely of lavender.

💡 THE PAINTER'S TECHNIQUE: When you start a past story, paint the scene first with imparfait. What did the place look like? What was the weather? What were people wearing? How did things feel? ALL of this = imparfait.

Use #2: Emotions, Mental States, and Feelings in the Past

How someone was feeling, thinking, wanting, believing — these are states, not actions.

FrenchEnglish
J'étais très heureux / heureuse.I was very happy.
Elle avait peur du noir.She was afraid of the dark.
Il se sentait seul et triste.He felt lonely and sad.
Nous étions fatigués après le voyage.We were tired after the trip.
Tu semblais inquiet.You seemed worried.
Ils avaient envie de rentrer.They felt like going home.
Je croyais qu'il disait la vérité.I believed he was telling the truth.
Elle pensait souvent à son enfance.She often thought about her childhood.
On savait que c'était dangereux.We knew it was dangerous.
Il espérait trouver un meilleur travail.He was hoping to find a better job.
Je ne comprenais pas pourquoi il était fâché.I didn't understand why he was angry.
Nous voulions rester plus longtemps.We wanted to stay longer.
Elle détestait les araignées.She hated spiders.
J'adorais ce professeur.I loved/adored that teacher.
Il préférait travailler seul.He preferred to work alone.

💡 KEY CONCEPT: Emotions and mental states are ongoing conditions, not completed events. They existed as a backdrop to whatever was happening. That's why they take the imparfait.

BUT WATCH OUT: If an emotion STARTED suddenly (a change), use passé composé:

  • J'avais peur. (I was scared — ongoing state → imparfait)
  • J'ai eu peur quand j'ai vu le serpent. (I GOT scared when I saw the snake — sudden onset → passé composé)

Use #3: Past Habits and Routines

Things you used to do or would do regularly in the past.

FrenchEnglish
Quand j'étais petit, je jouais dehors tous les jours.When I was little, I used to play outside every day.
Elle se levait toujours à 6 heures.She always used to get up at 6.
Nous allions à la plage chaque été.We used to go to the beach every summer.
Mon grand-père racontait des histoires le soir.My grandfather would tell stories in the evening.
Ils mangeaient ensemble tous les dimanches.They used to eat together every Sunday.
Je prenais le bus pour aller à l'école.I used to take the bus to go to school.
On regardait des dessins animés le samedi matin.We used to watch cartoons on Saturday mornings.
Il fumait beaucoup avant.He used to smoke a lot before.
Ma mère faisait toujours un gâteau le dimanche.My mother always used to make a cake on Sundays.
Nous passions nos vacances chez nos grands-parents.We used to spend our vacations at our grandparents'.
Les enfants couraient partout dans le jardin.The children would run all over the garden.
Avant, je lisais un livre par semaine.Before, I used to read a book per week.

💡 SIGNAL WORDS for habits:

  • Quand j'étais petit/jeune... (When I was little/young...)
  • Avant (before / in the past)
  • À cette époque (at that time)
  • Autrefois (in the old days)
  • D'habitude (usually)
  • Toujours (always)
  • Souvent (often)
  • Parfois / Quelquefois (sometimes)
  • Chaque jour/semaine/été (every day/week/summer)
  • Tous les jours/matins/étés (every day/morning/summer)
  • Le lundi / Le dimanche (on Mondays / on Sundays — habitual)
  • En général (in general)
  • Normalement (normally)

⚠️ "Used to" and "Would" (habitual):
Both "used to" and "would" (when meaning habitual past) translate to imparfait:

  • I used to swim every day = Je nageais tous les jours.
  • Every morning, I would wake up early = Chaque matin, je me réveillais tôt.

BUT: "Would" in the sense of hypothetical/conditional ≠ imparfait → use conditionnel:

  • I would go if I could = J'irais si je pouvais. (conditionnel — different tense!)

Use #4: Ongoing Actions in the Past (Background Actions)

Something that was in progress when something else happened. The "was ___-ing" use.

FrenchEnglish
Je dormais quand tu as appelé.I was sleeping when you called.
Elle cuisinait pendant que les enfants jouaient.She was cooking while the children were playing.
Il lisait le journal sur le balcon.He was reading the newspaper on the balcony.
Nous marchions dans la forêt.We were walking in the forest.
Les élèves travaillaient en silence.The students were working in silence.
Tu attendais depuis longtemps ?Had you been waiting for a long time?
Pendant que je faisais la cuisine, il regardait la télé.While I was cooking, he was watching TV.
Les enfants jouaient dans le jardin pendant que leurs parents discutaient.The children were playing in the garden while their parents were chatting.

💡 THE "ZOOM LENS" ANALOGY:

  • Imparfait = you're zoomed into the middle of an action. You don't see the beginning or end — just the ongoing process.
  • Passé composé = you see the whole action from start to finish — it's a completed "block."

"Je lisais" = You catch me in the ACT of reading (ongoing, no end in sight)
"J'ai lu" = I read (started, finished — done!)

Use #5: Age and Time in the Past

Telling what age someone was or what time it was.

FrenchEnglish
J'avais 10 ans quand nous avons déménagé.I was 10 when we moved.
Il avait 25 ans quand il s'est marié.He was 25 when he got married.
Il était 8 heures du matin.It was 8 o'clock in the morning.
C'était le 14 juillet 1989.It was July 14th, 1989.
C'était un lundi, il était tard.It was a Monday, it was late.
Elle avait à peine 18 ans.She was barely 18 years old.
Quel âge avais-tu à cette époque ?How old were you at that time?

💡 Age and time are STATES, not events — they describe the backdrop, so they always take imparfait.

Use #6: Two or More Simultaneous Actions in the Past

Multiple things happening at the same time in the past.

FrenchEnglish
Pendant qu'il cuisinait, elle lisait.While he was cooking, she was reading.
Je conduisais et ma femme dormait.I was driving and my wife was sleeping.
Les enfants chantaient tandis que la mère souriait.The children were singing while the mother was smiling.
Il travaillait sur son ordinateur pendant que la radio passait du jazz.He was working on his computer while the radio was playing jazz.
D'un côté, ils discutaient ; de l'autre, les musiciens jouaient.On one side, they were chatting; on the other, the musicians were playing.

💡 Key conjunctions for simultaneous actions: pendant que (while), tandis que (while/whereas), alors que (while/whereas), en même temps que (at the same time as).

Use #7: With SI — Hypothetical / Unreal Conditions (Present)

Si + imparfait = "If I ___ (but I don't)" — unreal present condition.

FrenchEnglish
Si j'avais de l'argent, j'achèterais une maison.If I had money, I would buy a house. (But I don't have money.)
Si tu étais ici, je serais content.If you were here, I would be happy. (But you're not.)
Si nous pouvions voler, nous irions partout.If we could fly, we would go everywhere.
Si elle parlait chinois, elle trouverait un meilleur travail.If she spoke Chinese, she would find a better job.
Si j'étais riche, je voyagerais tout le temps.If I were rich, I would travel all the time.
Si on vivait sur Mars, la vie serait différente.If we lived on Mars, life would be different.
Si tu savais la vérité, tu serais choqué.If you knew the truth, you would be shocked.

Condition TypeSI clauseResult clause

Unreal (present)Si + imparfaitconditionnel présent
Unreal (past)Si + plus-que-parfaitconditionnel passé

We'll cover the last two result clauses in later tenses!

Use #9: Politeness (Softened Requests)

Using imparfait to make a request sound gentler and more polite — similar to English "I was wondering..."

Direct (Present)Softened (Imparfait)English
Je veux vous demander...Je voulais vous demander...I wanted to ask you...
Tu peux m'aider ?Tu pouvais m'aider ?Could you help me?
Je viens vous voir pour...Je venais vous voir pour...I was coming to see you about...
Vous avez cinq minutes ?Vous aviez cinq minutes ?Did you have five minutes?
C'est pour quoi ?C'était pour quoi ?What was it regarding?

💡 WHY does this work? The imparfait creates psychological distance — it's less direct, less demanding. Instead of "I WANT to ask you" (present = blunt), you say "I WAS WANTING to ask you" (imparfait = softer).

⚠️ Note: The conditionnel (je voudrais, pourriez-vous) is even MORE polite. The imparfait is an intermediate level of politeness:

  • Je veux un café. (Blunt — "I want")
  • Je voulais un café. (Softer — "I was wanting")
  • Je voudrais un café. (Most polite — "I would like")

Use #10: Reported Speech (Indirect Discourse)

When converting direct speech (present tense) to reported speech in a past context, present becomes imparfait.

Direct SpeechReported Speech
Il dit : « Je suis fatigué. »Il a dit qu'il était fatigué.
Elle dit : « Je veux partir. »Elle a dit qu'elle voulait partir.
« Il fait beau », dit-il.Il a dit qu'il faisait beau.
« Je ne comprends pas », a-t-elle dit.Elle a dit qu'elle ne comprenait pas.
« Nous avons faim », ont dit les enfants.Les enfants ont dit qu'ils avaient faim.
« Tu es belle », m'a-t-il dit.Il m'a dit que j'étais belle.

💡 THE RULE: When the main verb is in the past (il a dit, elle a expliqué), the verb in the subordinate clause shifts back:

  • Présent → Imparfait
  • Passé composé → Plus-que-parfait (we'll cover this later)
  • Futur → Conditionnel (we'll cover this later)

Use #11: Near-Misses / Almost Happened (with PRESQUE or FAILIR)

Describing something that almost happened.

FrenchEnglish
Un peu plus et je tombais !A little more and I was falling! (= I almost fell)
Sans toi, je ratais mon train.Without you, I was missing my train. (= I almost missed)
Encore un pas et il tombait dans le vide.One more step and he was falling into the void.
La voiture renversait presque le piéton.The car was almost hitting the pedestrian.

💡 This is an advanced literary/spoken use. The imparfait here implies "it was about to happen but didn't."

Use #12: The "Être en train de" Equivalent in the Past

When you really want to emphasize "was in the PROCESS of doing something."

FrenchEnglish
J'étais en train de manger quand il est arrivé.I was in the middle of eating when he arrived.
Elle était en train de lire un livre.She was in the process of reading a book.
Nous étions en train de discuter d'un projet important.We were in the middle of discussing an important project.

💡 Just as être en train de + infinitive emphasizes "right now" in the present, être en train de in the imparfait emphasizes "right at that moment" in the past. The simple imparfait (je mangeais) usually suffices, but j'étais en train de manger adds extra emphasis.

5️⃣ WHEN NOT TO USE L'IMPARFAIT ❌

❌ Mistake #1: Using Imparfait for Completed Actions

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Hier, je mangeais une pizza. (if the action is complete)Hier, j'ai mangé une pizza.Completed action → passé composé
Elle arrivait à 8 heures hier. (if stating she arrived)Elle est arrivée à 8 heures hier.Specific completed event → passé composé
Il partait lundi dernier. (if he actually left)Il est parti lundi dernier.Completed departure → passé composé

⚠️ THE TRAP: "Hier, je mangeais une pizza" is NOT grammatically wrong — but it means "Yesterday, I was eating a pizza (when something else happened / as a background action)." If you mean "Yesterday, I ate a pizza (and finished it)," you NEED passé composé.

❌ Mistake #2: Using Imparfait for a Sequence of Events

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Il ouvrait la porte, entrait et s'asseyait.Il a ouvert la porte, est entré et s'est assis.Sequence of completed actions → passé composé
Je me levais, prenais une douche et partais. (as a one-time sequence)Je me suis levé, j'ai pris une douche et je suis parti.One-time chain of events → passé composé

⚠️ BUT: If this sequence was a HABIT, then imparfait IS correct:

  • Chaque matin, je me levais, prenais une douche et partais. ✅ (Every morning, I would get up, take a shower, and leave — habitual routine)

❌ Mistake #3: Using Imparfait for Sudden Changes

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Soudain, il criait.Soudain, il a crié.Sudden action → passé composé
Tout à coup, la lumière s'éteignait.Tout à coup, la lumière s'est éteinte.Sudden event → passé composé
Un jour, elle décidait de partir.Un jour, elle a décidé de partir.Specific decision → passé composé

💡 SIGNAL WORDS that demand PASSÉ COMPOSÉ (not imparfait):

  • Soudain / Tout à coup / Brusquement (Suddenly)
  • À ce moment-là (At that moment)
  • Un jour (One day — when introducing a new event)
  • Une fois (Once / One time)
  • Finalement (Finally)
  • Puis / Ensuite / Après (Then / Next / After)

❌ Mistake #4: Using Imparfait with Specific Counts

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Je voyais ce film trois fois.J'ai vu ce film trois fois.Specific number of times → passé composé
Elle allait au Japon deux fois.Elle est allée au Japon deux fois.Specific count → passé composé

❌ Mistake #5: Using Imparfait for Present States

❌ Wrong✅ CorrectWhy
Je pensais que tu es gentil. (current belief)Je pense que tu es gentil.Current state → présent

⚠️ BUT: "Je pensais que tu étais gentil" (I thought you were nice) = past belief about past state → both imparfait ✅

6️⃣ THE COMPLETE PASSÉ COMPOSÉ vs. IMPARFAIT GUIDE

This is the most important section of the entire French tense system. Master this, and you master French past narration.

The Decision Framework:

║                                                                      ║
║  ASK YOURSELF THESE QUESTIONS:                                       ║
║                                                                      ║

7️⃣ SPECIAL CASES & ADVANCED USES

Special Case 1: "IL Y AVAIT" vs. "IL Y A EU"

Imparfait: Il y avaitPassé Composé: Il y a eu
There was/were (describing a scene)There was/were (an event occurred)
Il y avait beaucoup de monde à la fête. (describing the scene)Il y a eu un accident sur l'autoroute. (event that happened)
Il y avait du soleil et des nuages. (description)Il y a eu un tremblement de terre. (event)
Il y avait une boulangerie au coin de la rue. (describing location)Il y a eu une explosion. (event)

Special Case 2: "C'ÉTAIT" vs. "ÇA A ÉTÉ"

Imparfait: C'étaitPassé Composé: Ça a été / C'a été
It was (describing / commenting)It was (completed experience / result)
C'était bien, le film. (commenting on what the film was like)Ça a été difficile, cet examen. (the experience was difficult — completed)
C'était magnifique ! (description/reaction to an ongoing experience)Ça a été une belle aventure. (summing up a completed experience)
C'était une époque formidable. (describing a period)Ça a été le moment le plus heureux de ma vie. (identifying a specific completed moment)

💡 In practice: "C'était" is used far more frequently. "Ça a été" emphasizes the experience as a completed whole. Many French speakers use them somewhat interchangeably in casual speech, but the distinction matters in writing.

Special Case 3: DEPUIS + IMPARFAIT (Action That HAD BEEN Happening)

Just as depuis + présent = "have been ___-ing" (present):
depuis + imparfait = "had been ___-ing" (past)

French (IMPARFAIT + depuis)English (Past Perfect Continuous)
J'attendais depuis une heure quand il est arrivé.I had been waiting for an hour when he arrived.
Elle habitait là depuis 10 ans quand elle a déménagé.She had been living there for 10 years when she moved.
Il pleuvait depuis trois jours.It had been raining for three days.
Nous travaillions ensemble depuis 2015 à cette époque.We had been working together since 2015 at that time.
Je l'attendais depuis midi.I had been waiting for him/her since noon.

💡 The pattern:

  • Depuis + présent = still happening NOW (J'habite ici depuis 5 ans.)
  • Depuis + imparfait = was still happening at that past moment (J'habitais là depuis 5 ans quand j'ai déménagé.)

Special Case 4: Imparfait for REPORTED SPEECH (Discours Indirect)

When the reporting verb is in the past, present tense in direct speech becomes imparfait:

Direct SpeechReported Speech
« J'ai faim. »Il a dit qu'il avait faim. (He said he was hungry.)
« Il fait beau. »Elle a remarqué qu'il faisait beau. (She noticed it was nice out.)
« Je ne comprends pas. »J'ai dit que je ne comprenais pas. (I said I didn't understand.)
« Nous voulons partir. »Ils ont annoncé qu'ils voulaient partir. (They announced they wanted to leave.)
« Tu es en retard. »Elle m'a dit que j'étais en retard. (She told me I was late.)
« Il pleut beaucoup. »Le météorologue a dit qu'il pleuvait beaucoup. (The meteorologist said it was raining a lot.)

Special Case 5: Imparfait after "COMME SI" (As if)

After comme si (as if), ALWAYS use imparfait (for present unreal comparison) or plus-que-parfait (for past unreal comparison):

FrenchEnglish
Il parle **comme s'**il savait tout.He talks as if he knew everything.
Elle me regarde comme si j'étais fou.She looks at me as if I were crazy.
Tu agis comme si rien ne s'était passé.You act as if nothing had happened. (PQP — we'll cover this later)
Il dépense **comme s'**il avait des millions.He spends as if he had millions.
Elle sourit comme si tout allait bien.She smiles as if everything were fine.

⚠️ RULE: After comme si, NEVER use conditionnel or présent:

  • ✅ comme si j'étais (imparfait)
  • ❌ comme si je serais (conditionnel — WRONG!)
  • ❌ comme si je suis (présent — WRONG!)

8️⃣ TECHNIQUES & MEMORY TIPS 🧠

Technique 1: The "WAS ___-ING" Test

If you can say "was ___-ing" in English and it makes sense, you probably need imparfait:

  • "I was eating" → je mangeais ✅
  • "She was sleeping" → elle dormait ✅
  • "They were playing" → ils jouaient ✅

BUT be careful: Not everything that uses simple past in English is passé composé!

  • "I was tired" → j'étais fatigué (imparfait — state!)
  • "She had blue eyes" → elle avait les yeux bleus (imparfait — description!)
  • "He knew the answer" → il savait la réponse (imparfait — ongoing knowledge!)

💡 EXPANDED TEST: Ask TWO questions:

  1. Can I say "was ___-ing"? → If YES → imparfait
  2. Can I say "used to ___"? → If YES → imparfait
  3. If NEITHER works and it's a completed event → passé composé

Technique 7: The "CHAPTER 1 vs. CHAPTER 2" Method

When writing or telling a past story, think in two phases:

CHAPTER 1 (Scene Setting) — IMPARFAIT:

CHAPTER 2 (What Happened) — PASSÉ COMPOSÉ:

Now tell us what happened. What changed? What events occurred? What was the sequence?

Trap #6: "When" Can Trigger Either Tense!

QUAND + Imparfait = "When" as a PERIOD (background):

FrenchEnglish
Quand j'étais jeune, j'aimais le chocolat.When I was young, I liked chocolate. (period of life)
Quand il pleuvait, nous restions à la maison.When(ever) it rained, we stayed home. (habitual)

QUAND + Passé Composé = "When" as a MOMENT (event):

FrenchEnglish
Quand je suis arrivé, tout le monde dormait.When I arrived (event), everyone was sleeping (background).
Quand il a vu le résultat, il a souri.When he saw the result (event), he smiled (event).

💡 THE QUAND TEST:

  • "When" = "whenever" (habitual) → Imparfait on both sides
  • "When" = "at the moment that" (specific) → PC for the event, IMP for the background

FormationNous present form (minus -ons) + -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient
Only irregular verbÊTRE → ét- (j'étais, tu étais, il était, nous étions, vous étiez, ils étaient)
Pronunciation-ais/-ais/-ait/-aient all sound the same: /ɛ/
Use forDescriptions, background, weather, emotions/states, habits, ongoing actions, age/time, simultaneous actions, si-clauses (unreal present), politeness, reported speech, comme si
Don't use forCompleted actions, sequences of events, sudden changes, specific counts, actions with defined endpoints

vs. Passé ComposéIMP = background/scene/ongoing; PC = foreground/event/completed
Meaning shiftssavoir: savais (knew) vs. ai su (found out); pouvoir: pouvais (could) vs. ai pu (managed to); vouloir: voulais (wanted) vs. ai voulu (tried to); devoir: devais (was supposed to) vs. ai dû (had to and did)
